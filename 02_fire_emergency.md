# Fire Emergency Protocol (ISM-EMRG-002)

## Purpose
Define the immediate response and command structure for any fire onboard, in line with
SOLAS Chapter II-2 and the company Safety Management System.

## Initial Discovery
Any crew member discovering a fire shall immediately:
1. Raise the alarm by the nearest manual call point or by shouting "FIRE FIRE FIRE"
   followed by the location.
2. Notify the bridge by any available means (VHF, internal phone, runner).
3. Attempt first aid firefighting only if safe and trained, using the nearest portable
   extinguisher of appropriate class.

## Bridge Response
On receiving the fire alarm, the Officer of the Watch shall:
- Sound the general alarm (seven short blasts followed by one long).
- Make a PA announcement: "Fire, fire, fire — location."
- Notify the Master.
- Stop main engine and adjust heading to bring fire downwind if practicable.
- Close all watertight and fire doors remotely.
- Activate the relevant fire main pumps.

## Engine Room Fire
If the fire is in the engine room and cannot be controlled by portable means:
1. Evacuate all personnel from the engine room and account for them at the muster station.
2. Stop fuel pumps and close quick-closing fuel valves from the remote station.
3. Stop ventilation fans and close engine room dampers.
4. Activate the fixed CO2 suppression system only after confirming the engine room is empty
   and the bridge has authorized release.
5. Maintain boundary cooling on bulkheads adjacent to the engine room.

## CO2 Release Authorization
The fixed CO2 system may only be released by:
- The Master, or
- The Chief Engineer with the Master's verbal authorization, or
- The senior surviving officer if the bridge is incapacitated.

Before release: confirm a head count of all engine room personnel at the muster station,
sound the CO2 pre-alarm for at least 20 seconds, and ensure all openings are closed.

## Accommodation Fire
- Send a fire team in BA (breathing apparatus) with charged hose to attack from windward.
- Send a backup team to provide boundary cooling on adjacent cabins and decks above/below.
- Account for all crew at muster stations before any compartment is closed.

## After the Fire
- Maintain boundary cooling for at least 30 minutes after extinguishment.
- Do not re-enter the affected space without BA and a charged hoseline.
- Notify the company DPA and flag administration as required by the SMS.
- Preserve the scene for investigation; do not disturb evidence.
