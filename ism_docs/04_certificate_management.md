# Certificate and Survey Management (ISM-CERT-004)

## Purpose
Ensure all statutory and class certificates remain valid, with surveys planned and completed
in advance of expiry, in compliance with flag state and class society requirements.

## Statutory Certificates
The following certificates must be valid at all times. Operating with an expired statutory
certificate may result in detention by Port State Control.

- Safety Construction Certificate (SOLAS) — 5-year validity
- Safety Equipment Certificate (SOLAS) — 5-year validity
- Safety Radio Certificate (SOLAS) — 5-year validity
- IOPP Certificate (MARPOL Annex I) — 5-year validity
- IAPP Certificate (MARPOL Annex VI) — 5-year validity
- ISM Document of Compliance (DOC, company-level) — 5-year validity
- Safety Management Certificate (SMC, vessel-level) — 5-year validity
- ISSC International Ship Security Certificate — 5-year validity
- Load Line Certificate — 5-year validity
- Tonnage Certificate — no expiry, but reissued on major modification

## Renewal Window
- Annual surveys: window is three months either side of the anniversary date.
- Intermediate surveys: between the second and third anniversary.
- Renewal surveys: within the three months prior to expiry.
- The DPA shall maintain a fleet-wide certificate tracker and circulate a 90-day expiry
  forecast to all Masters and Superintendents on the first working day of each month.

## Master's Responsibility
- Verify all certificates onboard at every change of command.
- Notify the office at least 90 days before any certificate expiry.
- Ensure copies are filed in the Master's safe and originals in the certificate folder
  on the bridge.

## Office Responsibility
- Schedule surveys with class and flag at least 60 days in advance.
- Confirm port and berth availability for surveys requiring drydocking or in-water survey.
- File renewed certificates with the Designated Person Ashore (DPA) within 7 days of issue.

## Non-Compliance
If a certificate expires while the vessel is at sea, the Master shall:
1. Notify the office and DPA immediately.
2. Notify flag administration and request a short-term extension.
3. Avoid entering port until the extension is granted in writing.
4. Record the event in the Deck Logbook and as a non-conformity in the SMS.
