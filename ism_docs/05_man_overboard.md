# Man Overboard Procedure (ISM-EMRG-005)

## Purpose
Maximize the probability of recovering a person fallen overboard, alive and uninjured.
The first 60 seconds determine the outcome.

## Immediate Actions by the Person Sighting
1. Shout "MAN OVERBOARD — port/starboard side" loudly and continuously.
2. Throw the nearest lifebuoy with self-igniting light and smoke into the water,
   as close to the casualty as possible without striking them.
3. Maintain visual contact at all times. Do not look away. Point continuously.
4. Send a runner to the bridge if no other communication is available.

## Bridge Actions
On hearing the alarm, the Officer of the Watch shall:
1. Press the MOB button on the GPS to mark the position.
2. Execute the Williamson turn (or Anderson if conditions favor it):
   - Williamson: hard rudder to the side of the casualty, swing 60° from original course,
     then hard rudder the opposite way and steady on the reciprocal course.
3. Sound three prolonged blasts on the whistle and the general alarm.
4. Notify the Master.
5. Broadcast a DSC distress alert and MAYDAY on VHF Channel 16.
6. Hoist the "Oscar" flag by day; display two pulsating red lights by night.

## Crew Mustering
The general alarm musters the crew at their MOB stations. The rescue boat crew shall
prepare the rescue boat for launching within 5 minutes of the alarm.

## Rescue Boat Operations
- Rescue boat shall not be launched until the vessel is brought to a near-stop with the
  casualty on the lee side.
- Boat crew wear immersion suits and lifejackets at all times.
- The bridge maintains continuous communication with the rescue boat by handheld VHF.
- A scrambling net or rescue cradle shall be ready on the lee side for direct recovery
  if the casualty drifts close to the vessel.

## Recovery and Care
- On recovery, treat the casualty as a hypothermia case regardless of water temperature.
- Move to a warm cabin, remove wet clothing, wrap in blankets.
- Do not rub the limbs.
- Notify the Company Medical Officer or call CIRM/TMAS for medical advice by radio.
- Continue first aid until professional medical care is reached.

## After the Incident
- Issue a "Pan Pan cancel" message on VHF if the broadcast was made.
- Submit a full incident report to the office and flag within 24 hours.
- Conduct a debrief with all hands to identify lessons learned.
- Update the SMS as required by the corrective action.
