# Fuel Oil Transfer Procedure (ISM-FUEL-001)

## Purpose
Ensure safe transfer of fuel oil between tanks, from bunker barge to vessel, or vessel to vessel,
in accordance with MARPOL Annex I and the company Safety Management System.

## Pre-Transfer Checklist
1. Confirm Chief Engineer and Master have signed off on the transfer plan.
2. Verify the SOPEP locker is accessible and contents are within service date.
3. Confirm scuppers are plugged and drip trays are in position under all manifold flanges.
4. Display the "Bravo" (red) flag by day or all-round red light by night.
5. Inform port control by VHF before commencing transfer.
6. Record initial tank soundings in the Oil Record Book Part I.

## Valve Line-up
- Open the receiving tank's filling valve last, after all line valves are confirmed open.
- Close the manifold valve before pump start.
- Verify the high-level alarm has been tested within the last 24 hours.
- Two crew members must independently confirm the valve line-up; one signs as "operator,"
  the other as "checker."

## During Transfer
- Maintain continuous watch at the manifold and at the receiving tank gauge.
- Start at minimum pump rate (≤ 30% capacity) for the first 10 minutes.
- Increase to full rate only after confirming flow path is correct and no leaks are observed.
- Stop transfer at 90% tank capacity; top off manually to 95%. Never exceed 95%.
- Maintain VHF contact with the bunker barge at all times.

## Post-Transfer
1. Close the manifold valve before stopping the pump.
2. Drain manifold lines to the slop tank.
3. Cap and seal the manifold flange.
4. Take final soundings; record received quantity in the Oil Record Book.
5. Sign and retain the Bunker Delivery Note (BDN) for at least three years.
6. Retain the bunker sample sealed for 12 months.

## Emergency Stop
If a leak, overflow, or fire is observed, the watchkeeper shall immediately:
- Press the emergency stop button at the manifold.
- Sound the general alarm.
- Notify the Master and Chief Engineer.
- Deploy SOPEP equipment if oil has reached deck or sea.
