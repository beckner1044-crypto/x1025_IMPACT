# Daily Vessel Reporting Procedure (ISM-OPS-003)

## Purpose
Standardize the daily noon, arrival, and departure reports submitted to the office and
charterers, ensuring data quality for performance monitoring and charter party compliance.

## Noon Report
The noon report is submitted by the Master every day at 1200 hours ship's local time,
regardless of whether the vessel is at sea, at anchor, or in port.

### Required Fields
- Vessel name and IMO number
- Date and time (UTC and local)
- Position (latitude, longitude)
- Course and speed over ground
- Distance covered in the last 24 hours
- Average speed through water
- Wind force (Beaufort) and direction
- Sea state and swell
- Fuel ROB (Remaining On Board) for each grade: HFO, MGO, LSFO
- Fuel consumed in the last 24 hours, broken down by main engine, auxiliary engines, and boiler
- Cylinder oil and lube oil ROB
- Fresh water ROB and produced
- Distance to next waypoint and ETA at next port
- Slip percentage and propeller RPM (24-hour average)
- Charter party speed and consumption order in effect

## Arrival Report
Submitted within one hour of the End of Sea Passage. Includes:
- All noon report fields, calculated from last noon to EOSP
- EOSP time and position
- Pilot boarding time
- All-fast time at berth or anchor
- Total voyage distance and time from COSP to EOSP
- Total voyage fuel consumption and average daily consumption
- Average speed achieved versus charter party warranted speed

## Departure Report
Submitted within one hour of the Commencement of Sea Passage:
- Last port and berth
- Cargo loaded or discharged (quantity, grade, BOL date)
- Drafts forward and aft, displacement, deadweight utilized
- Fuel ROB at COSP
- COSP time and position
- Next port, distance, and ETA
- Speed and consumption order from charterers

## Data Quality
- All times shall be in UTC for the report header; ship's local time may be shown in parentheses.
- Positions shall be expressed to one decimal minute.
- Fuel quantities shall be in metric tons to one decimal place.
- The Master is responsible for the accuracy and timeliness of all reports.
- Reports shall be transmitted via the company reporting platform; email is a fallback only.
